What It's Called

Two related concepts that describe how to be visible in AI-powered search

AEO

Answer Engine Optimization

Making your website's information easy for AI systems to understand and quote in their answers.

When someone asks ChatGPT a question about music lessons, AEO helps ensure your information gets included in the response.

Think of it like: Writing a clear, well-organized brochure that anyone can quickly reference and quote accurately.
GEO

Generative Engine Optimization

Structuring your content so AI systems recognize your organization as authoritative and trustworthy on specific topics.

When AI decides which sources to recommend for "nonprofit music schools in NH," GEO helps NCMS rise to the top.

Think of it like: Being the expert the reporter always calls for quotes‚Äîbecause you've consistently provided clear, reliable information.

"Traditional SEO asked: 'How do we rank higher in Google's list?' AEO/GEO asks: 'How do we become the answer?'"

Reasonable Questions

Addressing common concerns about AI and this approach

?

"Isn't AI just a fad?"

Why invest in something that might not last?

AI-powered search is already built into products people use daily: Siri, Google (AI Overviews), Bing (Copilot), and standalone tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity. Apple, Google, Microsoft, and Amazon are all investing billions in this technology. The question isn't whether this will continue‚Äîit's how quickly it will become the default way people find information. The good news: the work we do for AEO/GEO also improves our traditional search presence, so nothing is wasted.

?

"We're a small nonprofit. Does this really apply to us?"

Isn't this more for big companies?

Actually, local nonprofits may benefit more from this shift. In traditional search, you compete against every music school website in the country for keywords. In AI search, when someone asks about "music lessons in Nashua," the AI looks specifically for local, authoritative sources. A 40-year-old nonprofit with deep community ties and a scholarship program is exactly the kind of organization AI wants to recommend‚Äîif it can find your information. The catch: right now, most small organizations haven't done this work. Early movers get noticed first.

?

"What about AI getting things wrong?"

What if AI misrepresents us?

This is a real concern‚Äîand exactly why structured data (schema markup) matters. When you provide clear, machine-readable information about your organization, AI systems are far more likely to get the facts right. It's like the difference between having AI summarize a conversation it overheard versus reading directly from your official fact sheet. AEO/GEO is essentially giving AI a reliable source to quote.

?

"Is this expensive or technically complicated?"

Do we need to hire AI experts?

The foundational work is surprisingly accessible. Schema markup (the code that makes your site AI-readable) can be added to Squarespace through their existing Code Injection feature. The content work is mostly about organization and clarity, not AI expertise. This is well within reach for existing staff.

What NCMS Can Do

Practical steps to ensure NCMS is visible when families and donors use AI to find music education resources

1

Add Schema Markup

Give AI systems a reliable "fact sheet" about NCMS

  • Organization info (name, address, mission, history)
  • Programs offered (lessons, therapy, camps)
  • FAQ content (common questions, scholarships)
  • Events (recitals, concerts, open houses)
Implementation: Copy/paste into Squarespace
2

Structure Content Clearly

Organize information so AI can easily find and quote it

  • Add FAQ sections to key pages
  • Make scholarship info prominent and specific
  • Include concrete impact numbers (students, scholarships, years)
  • Use clear headings and direct language
Implementation: Content updates on existing pages
3

Build Citations

Get mentioned on other trusted websites

  • Claim/update Google Business Profile
  • Ensure consistency across directories
  • Pursue local press and community mentions
  • Partner organization cross-links
Implementation: Ongoing relationship building
