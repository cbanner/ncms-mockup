Common Questions

What age can students start?
We offer programs for all ages, from early childhood music classes (ages 0-5) through adult learners. Private lessons typically begin around age 5-6 depending on the instrument.
Do I need to own an instrument?
Not at first! We can recommend rental programs and help you choose the right instrument. Some instruments are available to borrow during lessons.
How much do lessons cost?
Rates vary by lesson length (30, 45, or 60 minutes). We offer scholarships for families who qualify, ensuring music education is accessible to all.
What if I need to reschedule?
We understand life happens. With 24-hour notice, lessons can be rescheduled. See our full policies in the Resources section.

About Private Lessons

Private lessons are the cornerstone of music education at NCMS. In a one-on-one setting, your teacher designs each lesson around your unique goals, whether you're preparing for auditions, learning to play your favorite songs, or exploring music for the first time.

Lessons are available in 30, 45, or 60-minute formats. Most beginners start with 30-minute lessons, while advancing students often move to longer sessions as their stamina and repertoire grow.

Template System Summary

These four templates create a consistent user experience across the site while serving distinct purposes: enrollment conversion (New Student), program exploration (Program Detail), community access (Scholarship), and fundraising (Donation). Each template can be adapted for similar page types throughout the site.
